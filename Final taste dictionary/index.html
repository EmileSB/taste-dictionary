How meanings are chosen

Corpus data : Japanese Web 2011 (jaTenTen11)

The dictionary focuses on meanings that frequently appear in native-speaker corpus data from Sketch Engines. For each word, we searched examples with the concordance tool on Sketch Engine and collected about 200 examples. We then categorized each into definitions. We then picked easy Japanese examples for Japanese learners, around JLPT N4/N5, for each definition.

Features of this dictionary

  1. The meaning are listed by frequency.
  2. Because users can be from any age group, meanings progress from simpler to more difficult ones.
  3. All meanings and definitions are selected from the most frequent uses found on Sketch Engine.
  4. The dictionary uses idioms that are relatively easy for beginners to understand, while more complex idioms are annotated.
  5. Difficult vocabulary is replaced with simpler Japanese where possible, for example, 採点 becomes 点数をつける.
  6. Furigana is provided for advanced kanji that are not taught in the early grades of elementary school.
  7. The example sentences include a wider variety of words and expressions.
